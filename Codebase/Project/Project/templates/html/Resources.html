Data Overview

Using a variety of different satellite that utilize different methods of data collection we collecting information in all the listed categories.

GROUND TEMPERATURES

Using NASA's earth data repository we utilized the measured ground temperatures collected from their MODIS terra satellite. By measuring the earths emitted infrared radiation, instruments on the satellite detect the different wavelengths using sensors. Algorithms back on earth then convert these waves into ground temperature estimates, accounting for effecting factors such a cloud coverage.

NORMALIZED DIFFERNCE VEGETATION INDEX (NDVI) AND NORMALIZED DIFFERENCE WATER INDEX

By utilizing Google Earths API we collected data points from their array of satellites both commercial from Maxar Technologies and government satellites such as NASA's Landsat and European Space Agency (ESA) sentinel satellites. Specifically using the ESA's Sentinel-2 this satellite monitors both the NDVI change and NDWI change by analyzing areas of reflectance of red, green and near infrared (NIR) light. With vegetation absorbing red light and reflecting NIR while water absorbs green light and NIR, the Sentinel-2 is ably to identify locations with high or low indexes and account for the changes.

SNOW/ICE CHANGE

To attain the change in snow and ice coverage we used planet-insights data from their Pelican satellite that was launched in 2023 as apart of the Skynet constellation. This satellite takes high resolution imagery paired with Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) to determine different surface types. SAR satellites emit microwave pulses and analyzing what is reflected back as surfaces like ice and snow reflect microwaves differently. This method is highly effected as it can penetrate clouds and fog for more accurate readings.
